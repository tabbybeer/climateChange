General Statistics

  1. Agriculture accounts for 10% of the EU's greenhouse gas emmisions
  2. Between 2001 and 2011, greenhouse-gas emmisions from crop and livestock production grew by 14%
  3. The demand for food is expected to grow by up to 70% in coming decades
  4. Livestock production is responsible for 65% of the Nitrous oxide gas production to climate change

How meat production is bad for the environment

Deforestation

Many areas of the amazon are being cleared for the production of meat both directly and indirectly. Often, forest areas are cleared using fire for the habitat of cattle farming and produuction of soybean for animal feed. The burning can also release huge amounts of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere - furthering the imapcts on climate change.

Greenhouse Gas Emissions

As seen previously, the farming industry contributes greatly towards greenhouse gas emission and meat consumption plays a large part in this. This is in several ways:

  1. The destruction of forest ecosystems.
  2. Animals produce large amounts of methane as they digest food.
  3. Decaying manure also releases large amounts of methane.
  4. The use of nitrogen-based fertilizers to grow animal feed produces nitrous oxide emissions.

Water usage

Beef is the most water-intensive food. It requires two times as much water than pork and four times as much water when compared to alternative protein sources such as lentils.
This is furthered because the soybeans for animal food are inefficient for farming when it comes to water usage.
On top of this livestock production contributes greatly to waterpollution because of manure.
